About Me

A Ph.D. candidate in Engineering with expertise in Technology Management, Economics, and Policy, specializing in applying advanced data-driven methods to analyze technological evolution, innovation dynamics, and global competitiveness, particularly within the digital economy. My research leverages econometrics, machine learning, and a novel application of phylogenetic network analysis (inspired by biological evolution) to uncover patterns in large-scale technological and economic datasets. I am particularly interested in exploring technological disruption, interdependence between technologies and institutions, and the socioeconomic impacts of artificial intelligence. My research utilizes diverse data sources including product information, patent records, and scientific publications. I am also deeply interested in the science of science, exploring the dynamics of scientific innovation and progress, as well as the application of artificial intelligence to understand and predict complex phenomena in technology, science policy, and digital economy.

Research Interests
